Popular Tourist
Attractions

Kufri is a tiny hill station in Himachal Pradesh, famous for its trekking and hiking trails. In winter, adventure-seeking travelers visit Kufri to enjoy skiing and tobogganing on snow-covered slopes. Kufri is also famous for its nature parks and picnic spots, making it a preferred location for couples and families alike.

In addition, Kufri's proximity to the hill station of Shimla makes it an important place to visit. Before you hit the road to Kufri, know important things like the best time to go, places to see, how to reach, and more to plan your trip more efficiently.

An Insight into
Kufri

Situated near the Himalayan range, Kufri is a picturesque hill station situated 20 km from Shimla in Himachal Pradesh. Founded in 1819 by the British, the popularity of the hill station as a tourist spot grew only in the last few decades. Kufri derives its name from 'Kufri' which means lake.

Especially popular among trekkers, Kufri is the starting point of treks to Manali, Shimla, Rewalsar and Fagu among others. Kufri, Shimla and Chail form the Golden Triangle of Himachal Pradesh. The cool and pleasant climate, along with stunning natural beauty, makes Kufri a perfect summer getaway.

Best Time to Visit

Summers (April-June) are mild, while winters (November-February) are cold. The best time to visit Kufri is in the summer, between April and June. You can enjoy panoramic view of the surrounding valleys and snow-capped peaks. However, you can experience heavy snowfall between December and February, and the snow-covered slopes of Kufri come alive with skiers and skiers. Monsoon season (July-September) is not recommended.
